# Why Your Side Project Will Fail (And How to Fix It)

*Published by Three Paws Media | March 16, 2026*

I've built 12 side projects. 10 failed. Here's what I learned.

## The Failure Patterns

### 1. Building in Isolation
**The mistake:** Coding for 6 months without user feedback.

**The fix:** Launch in 2 weeks. Get 10 users. Iterate based on feedback.

**Reality check:** Your perfect product is perfect only to you.

### 2. Solving Your Own Problem
**The mistake:** "I need this, so others must too."

**The fix:** Talk to 20 potential users before writing code. If 10 say they'll pay, build it.

**Reality check:** Your pain might be uniquely yours.

### 3. Feature Creep
**The mistake:** "Just one more feature before launch..."

**The fix:** Define MVP in 1 sentence. Cut everything else. Launch ugly.

**Reality check:** Users don't care about your features. They care about their problems.

### 4. No Distribution Plan
**The mistake:** "Build it and they will come."

**The fix:** Spend 50% time building, 50% time distributing. Start marketing before you launch.

**Reality check:** Great product + zero distribution = zero users.

### 5. Giving Up Too Early
**The mistake:** "No traction in 2 weeks, this is a failure."

**The fix:** Commit to 6 months of consistent effort. Most overnight successes took years.

**Reality check:** Compound interest works in business too.

## The Success Framework

After 10 failures, my 11th project made $5K MRR. Here's the framework:

### Week 1: Validation
- [ ] Define problem in 1 sentence
- [ ] Find 20 people with this problem
- [ ] Get 10 verbal commitments to pay
- [ ] Build landing page

### Week 2-3: MVP
- [ ] Build only core feature
- [ ] Ignore everything else
- [ ] Launch to 10 beta users
- [ ] Collect feedback daily

### Week 4-8: Iterate
- [ ] Fix biggest pain points
- [ ] Add most requested features
- [ ] Get first paying customer
- [ ] Document testimonials

### Month 3+: Scale
- [ ] Double down on what works
- [ ] Ignore feature requests from non-paying users
- [ ] Focus on retention over acquisition
- [ ] Build in public for distribution

## The Checklist

Before starting your next project:

- [ ] Can you explain the problem in 1 sentence?
- [ ] Have you talked to 20 potential users?
- [ ] Do you have 10 verbal commitments?
- [ ] Can you build MVP in 2 weeks?
- [ ] Do you have a distribution plan?
- [ ] Can you commit for 6 months?

If any answer is "no," fix that first.

## The Bottom Line

Side projects fail because we:
- Build without validation
- Ignore distribution
- Chase perfection
- Give up too early

Side projects succeed when we:
- Validate before building
- Distribute relentlessly
- Launch imperfectly
- Persist consistently

Your next project doesn't have to fail. But it will if you repeat the same mistakes.
