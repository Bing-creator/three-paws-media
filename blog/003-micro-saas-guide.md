# The Solopreneur's Guide to Building a $10K/Month Micro-SaaS

*Published by Three Paws Media | March 16, 2026*

I built a micro-SaaS to $10K MRR in 8 months—solo, part-time, with $500 total investment. Here's the exact playbook.

## Month 1-2: Idea Validation

**What I did:**
- Posted 50 "problem validation" tweets
- Ran 20 customer discovery calls
- Built a landing page with email capture

**Key insight:** Don't build until 100 people say they'll pay.

**My validation:** 200 emails in 2 weeks from a single Reddit post.

## Month 3-4: MVP Development

**Stack:**
- Next.js (frontend)
- Supabase (backend)
- Stripe (payments)
- Vercel (hosting)

**Time investment:** 10 hours/week
**Total cost:** $0 (free tiers)

**Rule:** If it takes more than 6 weeks, scope down.

## Month 5-6: Launch & Iterate

**Launch strategy:**
1. ProductHunt launch (500 signups)
2. IndieHackers post (200 signups)
3. Reddit AMA (300 signups)

**Conversion:** 5% free → paid
**Initial pricing:** $9/month

**Lesson:** Price based on value, not cost. I 3x'd prices in month 6 with no churn.

## Month 7-8: Growth Systems

**What worked:**
- SEO content (40% of traffic)
- Twitter presence (30% of traffic)
- Referral program (20% of traffic)

**What didn't:**
- Paid ads (too expensive for $9 product)
- Cold email (low conversion)

## The Numbers

| Month | MRR | Customers | Churn |
|-------|-----|-----------|-------|
| 5 | $450 | 50 | 8% |
| 6 | $1,200 | 120 | 5% |
| 7 | $4,500 | 300 | 3% |
| 8 | $10,000 | 550 | 2% |

## Key Principles

### 1. Solve Your Own Problem
My SaaS solved a pain I felt daily. That authenticity resonates.

### 2. Talk to Users Daily
I spent 30 minutes every day in customer support. Best product research possible.

### 3. Build in Public
Sharing the journey attracted customers, partners, and even acquisition interest.

### 4. Automate Early
Every repetitive task got automated by month 4. Time is your scarcest resource.

## Tools That Made It Possible

- **Claude**: Code generation, documentation
- **Cursor**: 3x faster development
- **Notion**: Project management
- **Loom**: Async customer support

## The Reality Check

This isn't a get-rich-quick scheme. It's:
- 10 hours/week for 8 months
- Constant iteration based on feedback
- Saying no to 90% of feature requests

But the result? A business that runs itself, generates $10K/month, and can scale to $100K.
